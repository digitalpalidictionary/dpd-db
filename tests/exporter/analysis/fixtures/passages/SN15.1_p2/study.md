# Analysis of: SN15.1_p2

anamataggo'yaṃ [anamataggāyaṃ] bhikkhave, saṃsāro. pubbā koṭi na paññāyati avijjānīvaraṇānaṃ sattānaṃ taṇhāsaṃyojanānaṃ sandhāvataṃ saṃsarataṃ. seyyathā'pi, bhikkhave, puriso yaṃ imasmiṃ jambudīpe tiṇakaṭṭhasākhāpalāsaṃ taṃ chetvā [tacchetvā (bahūsu)] ekajjhaṃ saṃharitvā caturaṅgulaṃ caturaṅgulaṃ ghaṭikaṃ katvā nikkhipeyya, ayaṃ me mātā, tassā me mātu ayaṃ mātā'ti, apariyādinnā'va [apariyādiṇṇā'va] bhikkhave, tassa purisassa mātumātaro assu, atha imasmiṃ jambudīpe tiṇakaṭṭhasākhāpalāsaṃ parikkhayaṃ pariyādānaṃ gaccheyya. taṃ kissa hetu? anamataggo'yaṃ, bhikkhave, saṃsāro. pubbā koṭi na paññāyati avijjānīvaraṇānaṃ sattānaṃ taṇhāsaṃyojanānaṃ sandhāvataṃ saṃsarataṃ. evaṃ dīgharattaṃ vo, bhikkhave, dukkhaṃ paccanubhūtaṃ tibbaṃ paccanubhūtaṃ byasanaṃ paccanubhūtaṃ, kaṭasī [kaṭasi kaṭā chavā sayan'ti etthā'ti kaṭasī] vaḍḍhitā. yāvañ'c'idaṃ, bhikkhave, alam'eva sabbasaṅkhāresu nibbindituṃ alaṃ virajjituṃ alaṃ vimuccitun'ti. paṭhamaṃ.

### English Translation

**Translation:** Monks, this transmigratory journey is without discoverable beginning. A first point is not discerned for beings who, obstructed by ignorance and fettered by craving, run on and wander through births. Monks, suppose a man were to cut down the grass, wood, branches, and foliage in this Jambudīpa and, gathering them together, were to make them into small pegs four inches long, and lay them down, saying: 'This is my mother, and this is my mother's mother.' Monks, the mothers of that man's mother would not be exhausted, even though the grass, wood, branches, and foliage in this Jambudīpa were to be used up and exhausted. Why is that? Bhikkhus, this cycle of rebirths is without a discoverable beginning. A first point is not known for beings who are hindered by ignorance, fettered by craving, and who are wandering and hastening through the cycle of rebirths. Thus, monks, for a long time suffering has been experienced by you, intense pain experienced, disaster experienced, and the cemetery filled. And just this is enough, monks, to be disillusioned with all conditioned things, enough to become dispassionate, enough to be released. The first.

**Literal Translation:** Inconceivable-beginning this, O monks, wandering-on. First end not is-discerned of-ignorance-hindered beings of-craving-fettered running-on wandering-on. Just as if, monks, a man, whatever grass-wood-branch-foliage in this Jambudīpa, that having cut, having gathered in one place, having made into four-inch four-inch pegs, should lay down: 'This [is] my mother, of that my mother this [is] the mother'; not exhausted indeed, monks, of that man the mothers of mother would be, then in this Jambudīpa the grass-wood-branch-foliage destruction exhaustion would go. That of what cause? Without-known-beginning this, O bhikkhus, wandering-on. Prior end not is-known of-ignorance-hindered beings of-craving-fettered running-on wandering-on. Thus for a long time by you, monks, suffering experienced, intense experienced, disaster experienced, cemetery increased. For as long as this, monks, enough indeed in all conditioned things to be disgusted, enough to become dispassionate, enough to be freed. The first.
