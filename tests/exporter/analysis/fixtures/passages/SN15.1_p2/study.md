# Analysis of: SN15.1_p2

### English Translation

**Translation:** Monks, this samsara is without discoverable beginning. A first point is not discerned of beings hindered by ignorance and fettered by craving, who run on and wander. Suppose, monks, a man were to cut down whatever grass, wood, branches, and foliage there is in this India, and having gathered it together, he would make it into four-inch pegs and lay them down, saying: 'This is my mother, and this is my mother's mother.' The mothers and grandmothers of that man would not be exhausted, monks, before the grass, wood, branches, and foliage in this India went to exhaustion and depletion. For what reason? Monks, this samsara is without discoverable beginning. A first point is not discerned of beings hindered by ignorance and fettered by craving, who run on and wander. For such a long time, monks, have you experienced suffering, experienced pain, experienced disaster, and swollen the cemetery. Thus, monks, it is indeed enough to become weary of all conditioned things, enough to become dispassionate, enough to be released from them.

**Literal Translation:** Incalculable-beginning-this monks, round-of-rebirths. First end not is-discerned of-ignorance-hindered beings of-craving-fettered running-on wandering. Just-as, monks, a man whatever in-this Rose-apple-Continent grass-wood-branch-foliage that cutting having-gathered-together four-inch four-inch pegs having-made should-lay-down, this my mother, of-her to-me this mother-thus, unexhausted-indeed, monks, of-that man mother-mothers would-be, then in-this Rose-apple-Continent grass-wood-branch-foliage exhaustion depletion would-go. That what-for reason? Incalculable-beginning-this, monks, round-of-rebirths. First end not is-discerned of-ignorance-hindered beings of-craving-fettered running-on wandering. Thus long-night to-you, monks, suffering was-experienced, intensity was-experienced, disaster was-experienced, cemetery was-increased. Long-enough-and-this, monks, enough-indeed in-all-formations to-become-weary, enough to-become-dispassionate, enough to-be-released-thus. First.
